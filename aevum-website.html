A Life Already Lived.
A Beauty Yet to Unfold.

Aevum was born from a home. For twenty-five years, Pratiti and Philip have lived surrounded by objects they rescued, reimagined, and fell in love with — a carved doorframe from a crumbling haveli in Shekhawati, turned legs from a rosewood cot found in a Chettinad warehouse, brass fittings salvaged from a colonial estate in the Nilgiris. Their home in New Delhi became, over the years, a living testament to the beauty of the second life.

The Turning Point

Friends who visited would linger. They would ask where things came from, how the pieces were made, whether they could commission something similar. The answer was always the same: every piece had been found by chance, designed by instinct, and built by hand. There was no catalogue. No factory. No supply chain. Just a couple with a shared eye and a deep love for materials that carry memory.

In 2026, Pratiti and Philip decided it was time to share what they had spent a lifetime building — not just the objects, but the practice. The way of seeing. The ability to look at a scrap of wood and imagine the treasure it could become.

What Aevum Is

Aevum is not a furniture company. It is an atelier for reclaimed luxury — a place where old palace doors become light fixtures, where bedposts become console legs, where a carved panel from a demolished temple finds its way into a side table that will be loved for another hundred years.

Every piece is personally sourced by Pratiti and Philip from antique dealers, carpenters, and salvage yards across India. Every design is theirs. Every object is made in-house by master craftsmen who understand that the beauty of reclaimed wood lies not in perfection, but in character.

The Name

Aevum is Latin for eternity — a long stretch of time, an age, a lifetime. We chose it because it captures the essence of what we do: we work with materials that have already endured decades, sometimes centuries. Teak that held up a roof. Mahogany that framed a doorway. Rosewood that cradled generations of sleep. These materials do not need to prove their strength. They have already done so. Our work is simply to honour that endurance and give it a new form worthy of the next chapter.

“We spent twenty-five years making beautiful homes for ourselves. Aevum is our way of making them for you.”
— Pratiti & Philip

From Fragment to Form

The making of an Aevum piece

Every Aevum piece follows a journey that cannot be rushed, replicated, or industrialised. It begins with finding — and it ends with a finished object that feels as though it has always existed.

01

Sourcing

Pratiti and Philip travel constantly — to old timber yards, demolition sites, antique dealers, and estate sales across India. They are looking for materials with character: old teak beams from demolished havelis, rosewood panels from colonial estates, carved fragments from temples and palaces.

02

Seeing

This is where Aevum begins. A carved wooden crest becomes the crown of a mirror. The turned legs of an antique cot become the supports of a console table. The design is never forced. It emerges from the material — from its grain, its scale, its history.

03

Designing

Every piece is designed by Pratiti and Philip themselves. There are no templates. No standard models. Each design responds to the specific fragment, the specific wood, the specific story. Dimensions are determined by the material, not the other way around.

04

Crafting

Our in-house team of master carpenters and artisans bring each design to life. They work with hand tools and traditional joinery techniques, honouring the integrity of the reclaimed wood. Surfaces are hand-polished to a matte finish.

05

Finishing

Every piece is inspected by Pratiti and Philip before it leaves the studio. They check for structural integrity, surface quality, and whether the piece carries the quiet warmth that makes an Aevum object unmistakable.

“We do not make furniture. We give forgotten materials a reason to be remembered.”

We Do Not Fell a Single Tree

Sustainability as a founding principle

Sustainability at Aevum is not a marketing position. It is a founding principle. Every piece of wood in our studio has already lived at least one life — as a roof beam, a door panel, a window frame, a bed. We do not source virgin timber. We do not contribute to deforestation. We work exclusively with reclaimed, salvaged, and pre-owned materials.

Our Commitments

  • 100% Reclaimed Materials — Every timber we use is sourced from old structures, estates, and salvage yards. No new trees are cut for Aevum.
  • Minimal Waste — Because we design around the material, our waste is remarkably low. Offcuts find their way into smaller objects.
  • Traditional Techniques — Our artisans use hand tools, natural polishes, and traditional joinery.
  • Local Craft, Local Economy — We work with carpenters in and around Delhi, preserving traditional woodworking skills.
  • Longevity as Sustainability — The most sustainable object is one that never needs replacing.

In a world of disposable design, Aevum chooses permanence. We believe the greenest piece of furniture is one that already exists — it simply needs someone to see what it could become.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Aevum?

Aevum is a design atelier specialising in reclaimed luxury. We source old, forgotten materials — teak doorframes, rosewood bed posts, carved palace panels — and reimagine them into furniture, lighting, mirrors, and décor objects. Every piece is designed by our founders and handcrafted in our New Delhi studio.

Are your pieces really one of a kind?

Yes. Because we work with reclaimed materials, each piece is shaped by the specific fragment it was born from. No two pieces are identical. Some exist as editions of one — once sold, they cannot be replicated.

What kind of wood do you use?

We work primarily with reclaimed Indian teak, Burma teak, mahogany, and rosewood — among the most enduring hardwoods in the world. We also work with brass, crystal, marble, and cane as complementary materials.

Do you ship internationally?

Yes, we ship internationally on a case-by-case basis. Please contact us for a shipping quote. International orders may be subject to customs duties, which are the buyer’s responsibility.

Can I visit your studio?

We welcome studio visits by appointment. Our studio is at 1 Windmill Place, Aya Nagar, New Delhi. Send us a WhatsApp message to schedule your visit.

Do you accept custom commissions?

Absolutely. We offer bespoke design services for furniture, lighting, mirrors, and more. Custom pieces typically take 6–12 weeks. Visit our Bespoke page for details.

What is your return policy?

Due to the handcrafted and unique nature of our pieces, we do not accept returns for change of mind. If your piece arrives damaged, contact us within 48 hours with photographs and we will arrange a repair, replacement, or refund.

How should I care for my Aevum piece?

Wipe with a soft, dry cloth. Avoid moisture, direct sunlight, and harsh chemicals. Apply natural beeswax once or twice a year. For lighting, use low-heat LED bulbs. Your piece is built to last generations with basic maintenance.
